The Present Tense of Irregular Verbs
Like most languages, the presence of irregular verbs always exist to make your language learning journey that bit more difficult. But, with practise, you'll be a master of both the regular and irregular verb conjugations!

Unlike regular verbs, irregular French verbs don’t follow the patterns mentioned on the previous page, so you have to learn the conjugation of each irregular verb individually.

As a beginner, it is important to learn the most common irregular verbs first because these are the ones that you will use the most. Then, as you progress with your language learning, you can move on to learn the conjugations of the rest of the irregular verbs.

The following verbs are the most common irregular verbs in French:

Avoir (to have)
Conjugation Translation
J'ai I have
Tu as You have
Il/Elle/On a He/She/It has
Nous avons We have
Vous avez You have
Ils/Elles ont They have


Être (to be)
Conjugation Translation
Je suis I am
Tu es You are
Il/Elle/On est He/She/It is
Nous sommes We are
Vous êtes You are
Ils/Elles sont They are


Aller (to go)
Conjugation Translation
Je vais I go
Tu vas You go
Il/Elle/On va He/She/It goes
Nous allons We go
Vous allez You go
Ils/Elles vont They go


Faire (to make/do)
Conjugation Translation
Je fais I do
Tu fais You do
Il/Elle/On fait He/She/It does
Nous faisons We do
Vous faites You do
Ils/Elles font They do
