Summary
As you approach the final page of my website, I would hope that you have grasped the basics of the present tense in French. Learning a language is far from easy. It takes time, practise and above all, patience! If you find yourself struggling or getting frustrated, don't give up because you'll get there eventually. Learning anything new is supposed to be fun, so make sure you find ways to enjoy what you're learning because it will keep you motivated along the way.

With the the present already under your belt, you can now move on to learn the remaining tenses and build up your knowledge of French vocabulary. As a final help, I have provided a short list of useful French phrases that you should learn to get you started.

Useful French Phrases for Beginngers!

- Yes/No - Oui/Non (wee/nong)
- Yes, please/No, thank you - Oui, s'il vous plaît/Non, merci (wee, seel voo play/nong, mair-see)
- Please - S'il vous plaît (Seel voo play)
- Thank you - Merci (mair-see)
- You're welcome - De rien (duh ree-en)
- Do you speak English? - Est-ce que vous parlez anglais? (essker voo pahrlay ahng-glay?)
- I don't understand - Je ne comprends pas (zher ner kong-prahng pah)
- I don't know - Je ne sais pas (zher ner say pa)
